A Dose of Normalcy
Good news, Jen. Not all things are crazy!
I realized that today after shirking off this sense of suspicion and paranoia I’ve been
carrying around. Maybe it will help you too. I’m just going to rattle off a few things
here, call it a mental exercise, call it what you will. It’s helped me calm down.
The car drove smoothly today. No engine hiccups.
It actually rained here last night, and the air has that crisp feel to it. I walked to lunch
from the office and passed by all these people who were out, people with normal
lives. I stood next to a guy at the crosswalk and listened to him chat to a friend on his
cell phone about GTA: San Andreas.
I brought home sushi take-out for Connie. She’s finally able to eat regularly again,
and I know she’s been craving it. When she gets really excited or happy, it’s like she
can’t talk, she just makes this high-pitched giggle-squeal and claps the palms of her
hands. I absolutely adore that about her.
Sushi = squeal-worthy. :-)
No sign of Sweatsuit Man. I think he’s gone.
So, yeah. There’s plenty of good things going on. I can’t let the bad thoughts creep in
and ruin the rest.
I hope you have some good ones, too.
And I’ll let you know when the FedEx guy shows up with the laptop.
